Dr Ian Fenech Conti

Dr Ian Fenech Conti completed his undergraduate studies in Information Technology—focusing on Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence—at the University of Malta. Soon after, he was invited to continue his work at the Department of Physics at the University of Oxford.

He pursued a Ph.D in Computer Science & Physics through a collaboration between the University of Malta and the University of Oxford, focusing on optical modeling and simulation. His research centered on developing cutting-edge image processing and correction algorithms for space telescopes.

Following his PhD, he joined the Institute of Space Sciences and Astronomy (ISSA) at the University of Malta as an Affiliate Lecturer—continuing to evolve his work and expand into new research areas, particularly in Artificial Intelligence and transform methods applied to radio astronomy. He also contributes to academic life by co-supervising MSc and PhD students, and occasionally delivering guest lectures on specialised topics.

Dr Fenech Conti has since transitioned into industry while maintaining an academic post. He now serves as Director and Head of Research & Product Development at Loqus Business Intelligence (LinkedIn), where he leads the development of advanced software solutions for route planning and optimisation. His work focuses on delivering high-performance, scalable tools for major delivery and retail companies, tackling the complex challenges of logistics operations at scale.

Research

My primary research area focuses on applying Computer Science and AI techniques to Astrophysics, specifically in the field of weak gravitational lensing.

For example, I contributed to the Kilo-Degree Survey (KiDS) project, where I was part of a team that developed a novel weak-lensing shear measurement pipeline that uses a self-calibration technique to correct for noise and weight biases—reducing shear biases to below 1%.

I have also worked on the Euclid Space Telescope mission, designing an innovative algorithm to model and measure the telescope's point spread function (PSF). This allows for precise correction of PSF effects in weak-lensing analyses, thereby improving our understanding of dark matter and dark energy.

Additional details of my work can be found on my Google Scholar and arXiv pages.

Logistics

I now work in the logistics industry, specifically on the software side, where I lead the R&D team behind a powerful optimisation platform for large-scale operations. Our product provides cutting-edge tools that help major companies plan, schedule, and deliver their routes and workloads efficiently.

We focus on advanced technologies and intelligent algorithms that can handle operations involving millions of daily transactions, ensuring that every job and route is fulfilled in the most optimised way possible—balancing performance, scalability, and real-world practicality.
